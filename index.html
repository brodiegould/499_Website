   360 No Smoke

Smoking is a problem, quitting is the solution.

According to a 2021 study in the Harm Reduction Journal, there is a global estimate of 81 million vapers globally. Of these estimated 81 million vapers, vaping is the most commonly used tobacco product among youth aged 13-25. Of those youth, the majority of them had reported never trying a tabacco cigerette in their life [1]. The most common reasons youth try vaping are for social reasons, and because of the flavours [2]. But with consumption going electronic, why hasn't quitting? The most common reason people 25 and older reported vaping was to quit smoking entirely. Another study conducted by the FDA in 2015 reported that about roughly 8% of smokers who wanted to quit were successful [3]. We believe that the reason smokers have such a difficult time quitting is because quitting plans rely heavily on willpower. With a more consistent quitting plan that removes willpower from the equation, it will allow this prodominantly young demographic to quit for good.

Design



Electronics

Using a 580mAh lithium polymer battery, an IRL540NPBF-ND N-channel MOSFET is used to provide 2.5A to the vape coil. A Uwell caliburn G/Koko Prime coil with resistance of 0.8-1.2 ohms is used to vaporize the nicitine loquid. By using the Seeeduino microcontroller, smoking is powered by sending a digital signal to the MOSTFETS gate. A PM-5R0H104-R 5V 200mF capacitor is used to supply steady power to the microcontroller when the device is in use to prevent power dropout.

Mobile

MIT App Inventor is used to compile block-based programming into its Java native components for deployment on iOS and Android devices. Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE) libraries are used to send data via service characteristics to the device. Additional libraries are used to generate and render quitting plots for each users custom quitting plan.

Embedded

The Seeeduino nRF52940 along with a real time clock (RTC) are used to receive bluetooth data from the app and control the device. The microcontroller recreates the quitting plan and generates two time intervals for usage. The first is the amount of time the user can use it in a given session, and the second is the amount of time between sessions. The further into the plan the user gets, the longer the duration between sessions get, just the same way the time for each session shortens.
